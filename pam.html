Nate's Framework for Effective Communication

My supervisor Nate introduced this framework during my internship at TTM Technologies. It emphasizes three key considerations before any communication:

1. Purpose

What is the goal of your communication? Nate advised: "Identify the purpose of the communication and then drive everything towards that purpose." Key purposes include:

  • Sharing information
  • Requesting action
  • Seeking clarification
  • Providing feedback
  • Building relationships

2. Audience

Who are you communicating with? Consider their:

  • Technical knowledge level
  • Role in the organization
  • Communication preferences
  • Cultural background

As Nate explained: "Communicating with a production floor operator is different from communicating with an external vendor."

3. Medium

What is the best channel for this message? Options include:

  • Face-to-face meetings
  • Phone calls
  • Email
  • Instant messaging
  • Documentation systems

Applying this framework helps ensure your message is clear, relevant, and delivered effectively. See a practical example below, or jump directly to the example.

Example from TTM Technologies

Element Consideration
Purpose Present a demo to a team of supervisors about a new data collection system.
Audience
  • Engineering team (technical details needed)
  • Production manager (impact on operations)
Best Medium?
  • Engineering team: Detailed real time demo of existing project
  • Production manager: Brief in-person update with integration plan
