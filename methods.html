Choosing the Best Communication Channel

During my internship, Joshua (EE Technician) emphasized: "Face-to-face is the most effective way to get things done." However, he also noted the importance of documentation: "You need to have it in an email or a text, so there's a record." Here's a guide to choosing communication methods at TTM:

Decision Guide

Method Best For When to Avoid
Face-to-Face
  • Sensitive topics
  • Complex discussions
  • Building relationships
  • Immediate feedback needed
  • When documentation is required
  • For simple information sharing
  • When participants are remote
Phone Call
  • Quick clarification
  • Urgent matters
  • When tone is important
  • Complex technical details
  • When written record is needed
  • Across multiple time zones
Email/Documented
  • Formal communications
  • Documenting agreements
  • Detailed technical information
  • Asynchronous communication
  • Establishing a clear record
  • Urgent matters
  • Sensitive topics
  • When immediate feedback is needed

Example from TTM

I remember a scenario where my manager Nate and I were communicating with another employee at the same site over email, and he was getting frustrated over a lack in clarity. I found it strange that we were using email for this issue considering he was in the other building. I thought face to face would have cleared up a lot fo issues with this matter, getting it resovled much quicker.
